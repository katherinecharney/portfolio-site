PROBLEM

Users need a personalized, relevant content experience within the Microsoft 365 creative ecosystem to enhance their productivity and creativity. With access to tailored, contextually appropriate templates, assets, and tools that align with their unique roles, industries, and preferences, user experience drastically improves.

HYPOTHESIS

To address the need for a more relatable and personalized content experience, we leverage secondary market research and surveys to develop persona archetypes. This approach ensures that content recommendations are not only personalized but also grounded in real user needs, industry trends, and behavioral patterns.

GOAL

The goal of this assignment is to create detailed personas for a product and lifestyle photoshoot using demographic, psychographic, and behavioral data. By providing key insights into user needs and preferences, the personas guide design, marketing, and business strategies.

MY ROLE

To ensure the final creative output resonated with target audiences and reflected Microsoft 365's core value proposition, I conducted secondary research including market studies and consumer insights. From this research I developed detailed use-cases for each persona, ensuring they were rooted in authentic user behaviors and needs.

UNDERSTANDING THE USER

In order to design and market effective products, understanding the target audience is essential. Personas are fictional, generalized representations of ideal users, which help guide design decisions and provide a clear focus on the audience's needs, goals, behaviors, and pain points. Developing accurate personas requires extensive research and empathy for the target users..

SELF-STARTERS

Individuals leveraging creator software for personal fulfillment, self-expression, and empowerment.

RESEARCH

These users are driven by passion projects and side hustles, like running a small knitting or fashion business. They use Microsoft 365 apps to organize their creative process and streamline business operations. For instance, they might use Microsoft Word to draft product descriptions, Excel for inventory tracking and financial management, and PowerPoint to create professional presentations for potential clients or collaborations. They turn to Paint on a tablet device to quickly sketch design ideas, experiment with color schemes, and test out new patterns before committing to more complex design tools.

RESULTS

These users can take advantage of Microsoft's All-in-One Ecosystem to manage designs, business operations, communication, and collaboration, all within a unified suite at a lower price point than separate, more expensive apps.

PROSUMERS

Millennials (aged 25-40), typically working in professional or knowledge-based roles primarily in tech, marketing, finance, consulting, entrepreneurship, and remote work positions.

RESEARCH

Prosumers are tech-savvy, early adopters of new technologies, and motivated by optimizing their work-life balance. They use Microsoft 365 for a range of personal and professional productivity tasks, including communication, project management, document creation and collaboration, task tracking, and scheduling.

RESULTS

These users can take advantage of Microsoft's All-in-One Ecosystem to manage designs, business operations, communication, and collaboration, all within a unified suite at a lower price point than separate, more expensive apps.

MODERN FAMILY

Millennial (aged 25-40) and Gen X (aged 41-56) parents typically managing multiple aspects of life, including family, work, and personal well-being. They work in both professional and knowledge-based roles, as well as those who may be part-time, remote, or entrepreneurial.

RESEARCH

They seek organizational tools to streamline their busy lives and improve family dynamics. These parents are comfortable with technology and seek practical tools that integrate seamlessly into their lives for better organization, communication, and time management. These users leverage Microsoft 365 primarily for scheduling, family coordination, task management, document sharing, and communication.

RESULTS
