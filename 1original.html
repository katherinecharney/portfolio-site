BACKGROUND

1Original is a general contracting firm specializing in residential and commercial projects.

PROBLEM

For companies with diverse product offerings, it's crucial to avoid overwhelming users with complexity. The goal is to create a navigation structure that ensures intuitive access to key services without confusion.

HYPOTHESIS

By employing clear, hierarchical menu options and multiple touchpoints, users can easily find their desired services through different entry points, ensuring a seamless, user-centered experience that encourages exploration without overwhelming the visitor.

PROCESS

When conducting competitor audits of real estate and general contracting firms, I leveraged Jacob's Law to identify universal features users would recognize, like hamburger menus, information architecture, photo direction and simple layouts.

VISUAL DESIGN

To ensure brand consistency, the visual design of the website was carefully crafted to align with the colors of Original Construction's existing logo. The color palette was selected to complement the logo's hues, creating a cohesive look across both digital and physical materials. This not only strengthens brand recognition but also establishes a professional and unified aesthetic throughout the site, enhancing the user experience while staying true to the company's established identity.

RESPONSIVE DESIGN

To enhance user experience across all devices, the layout and content adapt fluidly to different screen sizes, from desktops to smartphones, providing users with an optimal viewing experience regardless of the device they use. By prioritizing responsiveness, the site delivers a consistent, user-friendly interface, making it easy for clients to access CTAs and navigate the site wherever they are.
